About

Ari Atoll, part of the Maldives, features turquoise waters ideal for diving, with visibility often exceeding 30 meters. Its thriving coral reefs host hawksbill, green, and olive ridley turtles, frequently seen foraging or resting. The warm waters, averaging 26-30°C, support diverse marine life, making it a year-round diving haven. Beginners and experts alike enjoy gentle currents and sites like Maaya Thila, where turtle encounters are common, especially in shallow areas.

Cultural Highlights

Ari Atoll’s culture blends Maldivian traditions with Islamic influences. Local festivals like Eid feature vibrant music and feasts, while Bodu Beru drumming showcases rhythmic heritage. Handicrafts, such as woven mats, reflect artisanal skills. Resorts offer cultural experiences like Maldivian cooking classes, highlighting coconut-based dishes. The atoll’s serene ambiance merges with modern luxury, creating a unique cultural fusion.

Must-Visits

Dive at Maaya Thila, a renowned site for turtle sightings and coral pinnacles. Visit Dhigurah Island for whale shark encounters and beach relaxation. The Hanifaru Bay Marine Protected Area offers seasonal manta ray and turtle viewing. Stay at luxury resorts like Conrad Maldives Rangali Island for overwater villas. Explore local villages for authentic Maldivian life and markets.
