About

Sipadan, off Malaysia, features pristine waters with visibility up to 200 feet, making it a diving paradise. Its coral walls host green and hawksbill turtles, often seen resting or nesting, with over 20 sightings per dive possible. Warm waters (27-30°C) and strong currents enhance the experience at sites like Barracuda Point. A Marine Protected Area since 2004, it’s a year-round hotspot, peaking in July-August for nesting turtles.

History

Sipadan’s history began as a coral atoll, noted by the British as a bird sanctuary in 1933. Jacques Cousteau’s 1980s film brought global fame, boosting dive tourism. Designated a turtle egg reserve in 1964, it faced resort closures in 2004 to protect its ecosystem. Today, it’s managed by Sabah Parks, limiting visitors to 120 daily, preserving its natural heritage.

Must-Visits

Dive Barracuda Point for turtle tornadoes and coral walls. Explore Turtle Cavern for unique underwater caves. Mabul Island offers macro diving and relaxation. Sipadan Island itself, with permit, is a must for its drop-offs. Visit Semporna for cultural markets and boat tours to nearby reefs.
