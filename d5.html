About

Okinawa, Japan, offers clear subtropical waters with visibility up to 40 meters, ideal for diving. Its reefs host green sea turtles, often spotted at sites like Tokashiku Beach. Water temperatures range from 21-28°C, supporting year-round diving, with calmer conditions from June to October. Popular dive spots like Blue Cave and Kerama Islands provide gentle currents and frequent turtle encounters, especially in summer.

History

Okinawa’s history traces to the Ryukyu Kingdom (1429-1879), a maritime trading hub with China and Japan. Annexed by Japan in 1879, it endured the Battle of Okinawa in 1945. Post-war, it became a U.S. territory until 1972, shaping its modern identity. Today, it’s a dive tourism leader, preserving its unique cultural and natural legacy.

Cultural Highlights

Okinawa’s culture features Ryukyu traditions, with Eisa dances and sanshin music reflecting its heritage. The Shuri Castle, a UNESCO site, showcases royal history. Local cuisine includes goya champuru and awamori liquor, unique to the region. Festivals like Obon honor ancestors, blending ancient rituals with vibrant community celebrations.

Must-Visits

Dive Blue Cave at Cape Maeda for illuminated reefs and turtles. Visit Tokashiku Beach in Kerama Islands for turtle sightings. Shuri Castle offers historical insights. Churaumi Aquarium displays marine life, while Nakijin Castle Ruins provide scenic ruins. Explore Zamami Island for pristine beaches and diving.
